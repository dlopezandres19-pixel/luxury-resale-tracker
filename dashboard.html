Methodology & Notes

What is Value Retention (VR)? The ratio of current resale value to original retail price (MSRP). VR > 100% means the item sells above retail on the secondary market; VR < 100% means it has depreciated.

How each line is calculated. For each brand, VR is a weighted average by MSRP (handbags) or Retail Price (watches), meaning that more expensive items carry proportionally more weight in the index. This is the standard approach used by resale indices such as Rebag Clair. The chart shows the weighted VR per brand accumulated day by day; the table below each chart shows the latest snapshot, including the min and max VR observed that day as a measure of dispersion across items, and n = number of items in the sample.

Sample size (approximate, updated daily). Handbags: ~35 bags across 5 designers (Hermès ~10, Louis Vuitton ~9, Dior ~6, Gucci ~6, Saint Laurent ~4). Watches: ~150 references across 4 brands, filtered to icon models where possible — Rolex (Submariner, Daytona, GMT-Master, Datejust, Explorer), Cartier (Santos, Tank, Ballon Bleu, Panthère, Pasha). Hublot and Piaget currently run in fallback mode (🟡) due to model-name mismatches in the source — these use the top 5 references by relevance rather than the icon list, so their figures are indicative rather than curated.

Geographic coverage. Both Foxytotes and Watchcharts are US/Western-centric sources. Foxytotes aggregates from Fashionphile, Rebag, The RealReal, eBay and Vestiaire Collective; Watchcharts has global coverage but with US weighting. The tracker does not capture Chinese domestic resale (Plum, ZZER, Xianyu), Japanese pre-owned (Komehyo, Brand Off) or Middle Eastern private sales. Treat the VR numbers as indicators of Western authenticated resale, not a global benchmark.

Representativeness. Rebag Clair (the industry benchmark) processes transactions in the low hundreds of millions USD annually and its underlying transaction base represents well under 1% of global resale GMV. Foxytotes and Watchcharts combined are estimated to observe a broader but similarly US-weighted slice of the Western authenticated resale market. Values should be read as directional tendency indicators, not absolute market valuations.

Known limitations. (a) Gucci currently shows a flat line because Foxytotes applies a single 68% retention to all its Gucci bags, collapsing internal dispersion (this is a source-side limitation, not a scraper bug). (b) The history starts at the first automated run; early days will show few points. (c) The scraper runs daily at 23:50 Lima time (04:50 UTC+1) and commits the new data point automatically to the GitHub repository.
